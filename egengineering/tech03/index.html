Emerging technologies exploitable by EGEngineering


Electrical Impedance Tomography  - produces three-dimensional images of buried objects.  Utilizes measurements of resistivity, conductivity and “induced polarization” to distinguish material differences.  System is nonintrusive and is immune to magnetic interferences, which hamper current geo-physical technologies.  Current applications include UXO location, UST location, petroleum storage facility monitoring, landfill investigation, and archeological investigation.  US Navy interested in tactical applications.  Access through LLNL

Excitation of Mass tomography – produces two-dimensional images of breaches in non-conductive surfaces.   Imaging software renders image of electrical field passing through breach.  Suitable for landfill liners and caps and plastic piping.   May be applicable to metal piping.   Produces exact leak locations.   Best available technology provides “proximity” to leak.  Applications include landfill geomembrane integrity verification, PVC pipe leak detection, and reservoir integrity verification.  Access through LLNL

Pulse laser for surface contamination removal – laser emits light pulses that generate a plasma at the interface between a surface and the contamination (e.g. paint, epoxy, radiological).  System has been used by the DOE to remove fixed radiological contamination.   Surface temperature is less than 140F mitigating vapor emissions.  Suitable for clean removal of surface contamination with no damage to the substructure.  No additional waste stream from abrasive blast material (e.g. CO2, water, sand, slag).    Access through LLNL

Chemical weapon decontamination spray – The oxygenated gel is able to destroy all known biological and chemical agents.  The applied gel dries to a benign powder.   Primarily designed for clean up of WMD could be suitable for medical waste treatment.   Access through LLNL.  Advertised in the CBD.

Groundwater modeling tools - Highly detailed computer transport models enhanced by DOE’s research data.  Especially suited for radiological contamination.  Suitable for modeling of complex groundwater contamination and mitigation projects.  LLNL currently provides this service to municipalities (Orange County for El Torro NAS) nationwide.  Access through LLNL.

MTBE microbial remediation process – Mass generation of MTBE specific microbe.  Employed in bio-air filters or pumped into groundwater as vertical gradient barriers.   The application of a target specific microbe focuses effort on mitigating contamination rather than applying food to collocated microbes.  Initial field tests have been able to reach non-detect.  Private company developed air percolation process currently being evaluated.   LLNL inconjunction with UC Davis to offer licenses for the microbe and the growth process.

 Explosives – Recently de-classified energetic compounds providing more energy/workability, cost savings or environmental safety.   Potential application to shipscrapping or general de-construction.  Access through LLNL. 

Thermal UXO destruction – The “Fire Razor” is a combustion compound that burns at 4000K.  More cost effective than C-4.  UXO destruction application currently licensed.   Thimble size package burns through shell/mine casing and ignites energetic material.  Applications for shipscrapping and general de-construction.  Access through SAIC.

Plasma disintegration waste disposal process – Plasma furnace breaks down all compounds into elemental state.  Electromagnetic fields separate the elemental effluent.  Able to process hazardous and radioactive waste streams. Effluent separation process not functioning.  Large fabrication cost.
